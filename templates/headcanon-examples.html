Headcanon Examples: 50+ Ideas to Inspire Your Fan Creations

Need some headcanon inspiration? Whether you're writing fanfiction, developing a roleplay character, creating fan art, or just daydreaming about your favorite characters, sometimes you need a creative spark to get started. This collection of headcanon examples covers everything from wholesome comfort to angsty drama—organized by tone and fandom type so you can find exactly what you're looking for.

Use these examples as jumping-off points. Adapt them to your favorite characters, mix and match ideas, or let them inspire something completely new. Remember: there's no wrong way to headcanon!

Wholesome Headcanon Examples

These headcanons are perfect for comfort fic, found family vibes, and characters being soft with each other. They focus on the gentle, heartwarming moments that make us love characters even more.

  • They have a specific playlist they listen to when feeling overwhelmed, and it always helps them calm down
  • They learned to cook their best friend's favorite meal just so they could make it for them on bad days
  • They keep a small box of mementos from every significant friendship they've ever had
  • They give the best hugs because they hold on for exactly as long as the other person needs
  • They remember everyone's preferred drink order and always offer to grab coffee
  • They have a habit of leaving encouraging sticky notes in unexpected places
  • They're the person everyone calls when they need someone to listen at 2 AM
  • They always remember birthdays and put genuine thought into every gift
  • They practice positive affirmations in the mirror even though they'd be embarrassed if anyone found out
  • They save nice texts and messages to reread when they need a reminder they're loved
  • They learned their friend's native language just so they'd have someone to practice with
  • They always walk on the side of the sidewalk closest to traffic to protect whoever they're with

Funny & Quirky Headcanon Examples

These headcanons add humor and personality to characters. They're great for comedy fics, slice-of-life moments, and showing that even serious characters have silly sides.

  • They have extremely specific opinions about the correct way to load a dishwasher and will judge anyone who disagrees
  • They sing dramatically in the shower but insist they "can't sing" when anyone else is around
  • They've named all their plants and get genuinely sad when any of them die
  • They have a secret TikTok addiction they're deeply ashamed of
  • They always say "I'm not tired" five minutes before falling asleep mid-sentence
  • They're physically incapable of waking up without checking their phone first
  • They practice their "cool, casual reaction" in the mirror before important conversations
  • They've memorized the floor plan of every coffee shop they frequent for "strategic seating"
  • They give names to their cars/weapons/equipment and talk to them
  • They have a very specific niche interest they can talk about for hours if given the chance
  • They unironically own multiple pieces of merchandise from a "guilty pleasure" franchise
  • They rehearse important conversations in the shower and always win the argument there
  • They pretend not to know how to do certain things just so someone else will handle it
  • They have a drawer full of condiment packets they "might use someday"

Angsty & Dark Headcanon Examples

These headcanons explore pain, trauma, and complex emotions. They add depth to characters and are perfect for hurt/comfort fics, character studies, and exploring the shadows behind even the brightest heroes.

  • They never fully relax, always keeping part of their awareness on exits and potential threats
  • They apologize constantly, even for things that aren't their fault, because they learned early that being wrong meant being hurt
  • They flinch at raised voices even when the person is just excited, not angry
  • They have scars they always keep covered and stories they'll never tell
  • They lie about how they're doing so automatically they sometimes don't realize they're doing it
  • They have a recurring nightmare they've never told anyone about
  • They keep their loved ones at arm's length because they're terrified of what would happen if someone used them as leverage
  • They taught themselves to cry silently as a child and never unlearned it
  • They have specific foods they can't eat because of association with their worst memories
  • They secretly believe that everyone will eventually abandon them, so they try not to get too attached
  • They check that doors are locked multiple times before they can sleep
  • They keep a bag packed and ready in case they need to leave quickly, even though they haven't needed to in years

Relationship & Shipping Headcanon Examples

These headcanons explore dynamics between characters—romantic, platonic, or anywhere in between. Use them for ship fic, friendship content, or exploring character dynamics.

  • They always steal their partner's hoodies even though they have their own, because the stolen ones smell like them
  • They developed inside jokes so specific that no one else could ever understand them
  • One of them runs cold and one runs hot, and they always end up gravitating together on the couch
  • They have a shared Notes app document of restaurants they want to try together
  • They text each other when they're in the same room just to share things they can't say out loud
  • They have separate friend groups but their friends have all become friends too, creating one big extended circle
  • They argue about who was the one to make the first move because their memories of it are completely different
  • They have "their song" but it's something embarrassing they'd never admit to anyone else
  • They keep their important conversations for long car rides when they don't have to make eye contact
  • One of them always falls asleep during movies, so the other keeps a blanket ready

Anime-Specific Headcanon Examples

These headcanons play with common anime tropes and character archetypes. Perfect for shonen protagonists, magical girls, isekai heroes, and everyone's favorite anime characters.

  • The protagonist practices their signature attack pose in their room when they think no one's watching
  • The "cool" rival character secretly keeps every letter of encouragement they've ever received
  • The cheerful support character writes in a journal every night to process everything they keep bottled up during the day
  • The serious mentor type has an embarrassingly extensive collection of cute things hidden in their home
  • The villain has one person from their past they still check on from afar, even though they'd never admit it
  • The hotheaded character is actually really good with kids and becomes surprisingly gentle around them
  • The genius character sometimes pretends not to know things so other people can feel smart
  • The stoic character expresses affection through acts of service because they never learned how to say "I care"

Book & Literature Character Headcanon Examples

These headcanons work well for characters from novels, classics, and literary works. They explore the inner lives of characters whose stories we often only see from the outside.

  • They annotate their books with thoughts and reactions, then feel embarrassed when someone else reads the same copy
  • They have a reading spot where they go when they need to escape, and very few people know about it
  • They judge people by their bookshelves but try not to be too obvious about it
  • They've reread their comfort book so many times the spine is held together with hope and tape
  • They have intense parasocial relationships with fictional characters and are aware this is probably projecting
  • They write fan letters to their favorite authors but never actually send them
  • They can quote obscure literature in casual conversation and don't understand why people find this weird

Video Game Character Headcanon Examples

These headcanons capture the quirks of video game protagonists, NPCs, and the unique situations of game worlds.

  • They save obsessively even when the game auto-saves, just in case
  • They got emotionally attached to a minor NPC and now check on them every time they pass by
  • They have gaming snacks they only eat during specific types of gameplay
  • They've replayed their favorite game's prologue multiple times just to experience the beginning again
  • They name all their RPG characters variations of the same name across every game
  • They feel genuine guilt for in-game choices even when there were no real consequences
  • They have superstitions about gaming setups that they don't actually believe but follow anyway
  • They've 100% completed a game and work it into conversation more often than they should

How to Use These Headcanon Examples

These examples are meant to be starting points, not finished ideas. Here's how to make them your own:

  • Adapt to your character: Take any headcanon and ask "how would [character] specifically do this?" The details matter.
  • Combine ideas: Mix a wholesome headcanon with an angsty one for complex characterization.
  • Add specificity: Instead of "they have a comfort food," specify what it is and why it's meaningful.
  • Explore origins: For any headcanon, ask "why is this true?" The backstory often becomes the story.
  • Share and discuss: Post your headcanons and see how others react—fandom is more fun together.
